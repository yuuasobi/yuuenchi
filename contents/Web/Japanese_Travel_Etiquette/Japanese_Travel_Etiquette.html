Japanese Travel Etiquette

Welcome to Japan!

Three Principles of Japanese Etiquette

  • Respect for Quietness
  • Maintain Cleanliness
  • Do Not Disturb Others

Enjoy your trip respectfully and safely.

No Entry to Farmland

Proper Tourism

Enjoy the scenery from designated tourist spots and viewpoints.

Entering Private Farms

Do not enter private farms without permission. You may damage crops.

Public Transportation Etiquette

Luggage Placement

Place large luggage at the end and avoid blocking other passengers.

Priority Seats

Keep priority seats available for elderly and disabled passengers.

Loud Conversations

Keep quiet in the train and avoid disturbing other passengers.

Cutting in Line

Follow the order and do not cut in line.

Shrine & Temple Etiquette

Bow at Torii Gate

Bow slightly when passing through the torii gate.

Purification at Chozuya

Purify your hands and mouth at the chozuya before praying.

Noise in Main Hall

Keep quiet in the main hall and prayer hall.

No Photography Areas

Do not take photos in no-photography areas.

Cultural Heritage Protection

Respect Restricted Areas

Do not enter restricted areas.

Care for Cultural Assets

Do not touch or climb on sculptures and buildings.

Breaking Cherry Branches

Do not break cherry branches or pick flowers.

Waste Management

Take Trash Home

Take trash home if there are no trash bins.

Follow Sorting Rules

Follow sorting rules (burnable waste, recycling, etc.).

No Littering

Do not litter on streets or tourist spots.

Dining Etiquette

Stop to Eat

Stop to eat in no-walking-eating areas.

No Tipping

Tipping is not required (it's not a custom).

No Outside Food

Do not eat in no-outside-food areas.

Hot Spring & Public Bath Etiquette

Clean Before Bathing

Wash your body before entering the bath.

Hair Care

Do not let your hair touch the bath water.

No Items in Bath

Do not put swimsuits or towels in the bath.

No Loud Noise

Do not make loud noise.

Accommodation Etiquette

Keep Quiet

Keep quiet and avoid noise that disturbs hallways or adjacent rooms.

Respect Times

Follow check-in and check-out times.

No Smoking in Non-Smoking Rooms

Do not smoke in non-smoking rooms.

Queuing & Waiting

Follow Order

Wait in line following the order.

Vending Machines & Cashiers

Follow the same order at vending machines and cashiers.

Cutting in Line

Do not cut in line.

Photography Etiquette

Respect No Flash Areas

Follow no-flash rules in designated areas.

No Drone Zones

Do not fly drones in no-drone zones.

Unauthorized Photography

Do not take photos of others without permission.

Noise & Consideration

Nighttime Quiet

Do not make noise late at night (residential areas and hotels).

Speaker Volume

Do not play speakers at high volume on streets or in parks.

Smoking Etiquette

Use Ashtrays

Always dispose of cigarette butts in ashtrays.

No Street Smoking

Do not smoke in no-smoking areas on streets.

No Walking While Smoking

Walking while smoking is dangerous and prohibited.

Have a wonderful trip in Japan!
